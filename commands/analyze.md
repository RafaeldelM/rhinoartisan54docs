### <img src="../.gitbook/assets/OUT_WEIGHT_BLACK.png" alt="" data-size="line">Weight

This command will calculate the weight of our design, taking into account the weight of the metals and gems. In this case we only have one type of metal and diamonds, but in cases where we have several, it will show the complete list.

<figure><img src="../.gitbook/assets/image (1).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

In the case of default metal, it is the value shown in Outliner, so you can change it, and it will affect the weight, rendering, etc... You will find more information in the Outliner section.

<figure><img src="../.gitbook/assets/image (3).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

### <img src="../.gitbook/assets/WEIGHT_AREA.png" alt="" data-size="line">Weight by Area

This tool is ingeniously designed for calculating the weight of plating based on the surface area of an object. It proves immensely useful for designers and manufacturers seeking accuracy in metal coating processes, allowing for a more precise estimation of the metal weight used in electroplating.

<figure><img src="../.gitbook/assets/image (4).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

### <img src="../.gitbook/assets/ANALYZE_ANALYZE_THICKNESS (1).png" alt="" data-size="line">Thickness

With this command, you can see the Thickness of an object on a point you choose. It's a great tool to check if a creative design is printable on your 3D printer.

![](<../.gitbook/assets/image (205).png>)

### <img src="../.gitbook/assets/ANALYZE_ANALYZE_GLOBAL_THICKNESS.png" alt="" data-size="line">Global Thickness

With this command, you can create a mesh from an object, and set a specific minimum thickness for the resulting mesh.

![](<../.gitbook/assets/image (122).png>)
